Adapted Physical Exercises

Gentle exercise suggestions for each type of disability.

Back to Home

Physical Disability

Swimming to strengthen muscles gently and improve flexibility.

  • Swimming: A gentle exercise that helps strengthen muscles and improve flexibility without putting pressure on the joints.
  • Assisted Walking: A great way to strengthen leg muscles while increasing mobility.
  • Stationary Bike: Ideal for people with joint problems, providing low-impact exercise for the legs.
  • Gentle Stretching: A flexibility exercise that improves mobility and muscle suppleness.

Sensory Disability

Yoga and breathing exercises for mental well-being.

  • Adaptive Yoga: Gentle poses and breathing exercises to improve flexibility, concentration, and mental well-being.
  • Guided Meditation: Helps with mental relaxation and stress management, especially beneficial for individuals with sensory impairments.
  • Deep Breathing Exercises: Improves blood circulation and reduces anxiety.
  • Therapeutic Massage: Promotes relaxation and relieves muscle tension related to sensory disorders.

Intellectual Disability

Gentle and playful exercises like adapted dance and gardening.

  • Adapted Dance: A fun physical activity that improves coordination and memory while providing cardiovascular benefits.
  • Therapeutic Gardening: Promotes concentration, patience, and offers an excellent way to connect with nature.
  • Physical Memory Games: Fun activities to improve memory while staying physically active.

Psychological Disability

Assisted walking and meditation for mental relaxation.

  • Assisted Walking: A gentle physical activity that reduces stress and improves mental health, easy to adapt.
  • Mindfulness Meditation: Practice for anxiety reduction and stress management in individuals with psychological disabilities.
  • Muscle Relaxation Exercises: Techniques for relieving physical and psychological tension.
  • Therapeutic Yoga: Promotes mental balance and physical relaxation, ideal for reducing stress and anxiety symptoms.
